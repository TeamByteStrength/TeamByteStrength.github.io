What are your team’s rules?

  1. If you’re going to miss a meeting, notify the rest of the group via GroupMe
  2. Meet your work deadlines
  3. A meeting can only end if the agenda for the next meeting is set.
  4. All disputes must be resolved civilly.
  5. Decisions that affect the group are to be decided by majority vote.
  6. If you miss a meeting, you need to contact the rest of the group to find out what you missed.
  7. The group will stage an intervention to correct any member who is slacking or otherwise being antisocial before ejecting them.
  8. Evenly distribute work among team members.

What would your team like to do to help combat this problem / issue? Here is where you connect your plan to fulfilling a need of the organization.
The Boys and Girls Club exists to give children a safe place to go after school. Our team will be automating part of the organization’s daily operations, allowing its employees to engage in their primary objective - interacting with children.

What are your three SMART goals? (First goal is $100/person x # of people in your group. The other two are up to you).

  1. To achieve an average of 10 working hours per student on the project.
  2. Submit the project deliverables to the client for review and approval by November 30, 2017.
  3. Finish filming and begin editing the raw footage by November 1, 2017.

What is your plan for achieving these goals?

  1. Schedule meeting to determine filming location(s), times and group member roles
  2. Begin filming
  3. Finish filming, begin editing
  4. Submit project deliverables for review
  5. End

What is your rough timeline for achieving these goals?
Phase 1: 2 weeks (End date: 10.12.17)
Phase 2: 2 weeks (End date: 10.26.17)
Phase 3: 4 weeks (End date: 11.23.17)
Phase 4: 1 week (End date 11.30.17 due to Thanksgiving break)

What potential problems do you anticipate?

  1. Client rejects product
  2. Any given phase takes longer than anticipated
  3. Client doesn’t have necessary equipment
  4. Data loss - video is literally destroyed or lost
  5. Arranging meeting times with client is particularly difficult

What is your fallback plan? What is your “point of no return” date for implementing the fallback plan? (ex: “if we have not achieved 50% of our goal by midterm, we will switch to Plan B.”).
The group’s fallback plan is to decorate a tree for Baby Fold in Bloomington, IL.
The deadline for this plan is Friday, November 10, 2017, the last day our group can contribute to the event.
